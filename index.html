William Lager

I am a digital producer for MPR News. Over the last four years, I have provided digital support for various news projects, content delivery systems and the occasional reporting under my byline. I provided primary support for Ground Level, a community reporting project that had a mandate to experiment with story presentation and audience engagement.

I was part of the award winning projects; Betrayed by Silence, on Catholic Church abuse and cover-ups, our coverage of both Marriage Amendments in Minnesota, Climate Change, and many others.

I am currently involved in a number of projects that will be public in the near future.

Before joining MPR News, William Lager worked with various non-profit organizations, such as mnartists.org, an online community that bridged the gap between artists, organizations and the public before the rise of Facebook and other easy social media, and Forecast Public Art on the Public Art Toolkit, a digital resource that could live online and offline to guide artists and communities through the process of creating public art.

An incomplete selection of reporting projects William Lager worked on:

Ground Level

A testbed for reporting presentation and audience engagement. Everything was focused as mobile friendly, with early attempts finding lightweight browser based presentations to entice audiences to engage with the reporting. Working with editor Dave Peters and various reporters, we were able to experiment with varied technologies and techniques to bridge the gap with audiences.

Fighting for an American Countryside

People in rural Minnesota are battling small-town decline with determination, resourceful thinking, and unwavering belief. A multi-avenue presentation that meets the audience where they are.
